  1. Unit 1 Outcome 1
    1. Describing the purpose of IT in modern business.

      Information technology (IT) plays a vital role in today's business. From large to small scale businesses, we could see the omnipresent use of IT tools in many aspects of business operation: internal communication, data management and relationship management with external suppliers and customers.

      Businesses become more and more dependent on these tools with the development of technology and the recent trend of working from home. From the first thing in the morning starting with the online meeting, emails, to the data tracking and stock management, and live chat technology to offer a more instant customer service or online marketing, we cannot form a business without the application of information technology.

      IT technology brings innovation to the modern world and is essential to businesses. With the good use of technology, business can save costs and enjoy efficiency and success.

    2. Describing methods, skills and resources needed to complete digital projects successfully – what do you need to make projects successful? What tools? What skills?

      In order to complete digital projects successfully, first thing we need to understand what they are and what they consist of. Digital projects are, as it literally means, products that are developed and delivered on digital platforms. These are virtual products delivered as a service to the customers such as digital marketing campaigns, website design or software development. In general, like all other projects, digital projects begins with a plan, followed by the executing and testing process before the end product is produced. Plans help us knowing our purposes and expectations clearly. This is critically essential for the whole project to prevent us from going the wrong way. Once we have the plan we can go into execution to acquire the materials and apply the tools which are needed for the project.

      What skills do we need through these stages to carry out digital projects? As a project manager, communication skills, technical skills and analytical skills are equally important for a successful project. Project managers need to have good communication, the ability to use technical tools for the project, and a critical mind to analyse the timeframe and material used on the project. What tools do we need? The most fundamental thing is a computer and the software required for the project. For the resources, if we're making a digital campaign for a brand then we would need their online content, social media videos and images as much as possible to create a successful campaign.

    7. Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

      GDPR stands for General Data Protection Regulation which is designed to protect the data of individuals. After the new legislation introducing on 25th May 2018, there were some changes which would affect digital marketers in obtaining consent from users and data access. The users now have more control of how their personal data are used, which means when we collect information through customer's online activity for marketing purposes in a digital project, we face higher standards to obtain consent and users have the complete right to withdraw their consent for data processing. Also, reaching out to target customers by communicating directly through emails or instant messaging are also impacted by the GDPR.

    5. Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

      All websites are protected by Copyright Designs and Patents Act 1988 when they are set up. All the original content on the website including music, graphics, pictures, texts and videos are all under the copyright protection and the creator has the full right for the use and distribution. When creating websites, we need to be careful with the materials from third parties to prevent infringement of intellectual property. Also when naming your website, we should avoid using a name that has already been registered by a third party. Apart from the copyright, we should be careful with the comments we or someone else posts on the website. A defamation claim can arise if inappropriate statement found.

    6. Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using webpages and how we can overcome them

      People with disabilities have wide diversities in the problems they face. They might have difficulties with their hearing, sight, cognitive abilities, speech or physical activities. For people with auditory disabilities, video content with no captions or audio content without sound control to turn the volume up would not be ideal for them to use. Same situation with the video content without the audio alternatives or texts or images don't have a clear colour contrast with the background can be difficult for people who have visual disabilities to access. Content and page layout structured in a complex way which doesn't have a good navigation or easy to read sentences are barriers for cognitive disabled people. Any settings on the website with voice-only interactions is not being thoughtful enough for users with speech disabilities and websites without full keyboard support or with short time limit on tasks would make it hard to use for physical disabled people.

      To take all users into account and create a disability-friendly website, we should avoid the points mentioned above by improving the visual designs and content, and ensure the assistive technologies that disabled people use, such as screen readers and voice recognition software for text input can adapt well on our websites.

    7. Describe which are the appropriate filetypes for websites

      File types on websites that work out the best are: JPEG, PNG, SVG and GIF. JPEG or JPG is the most common choice for images, while PNG and SVG are not just suitable for images but also have more advanced functions and performance than JPEG. PNG files support texts and graphics and won't lose the image quality. SVG can be animated and create high resolution images and graphics. GIFs can't process pictures with too many shades like JPEGs but they can be animated and ideal for small icons and logos.
